**Empowering Communities: The CKICAS Framework for Adaptive, Real-Time Decision-Making in Sustainable Development**

Complex adaptive systems (CAS) theory provides powerful insights into emergent behavior, yet fails to account for intentional intelligence as an active shaping force. This paper introduces Constitutional Knowledge-Intensive Complex Adaptive Systems (CKICAS), extending CAS theory to intelligence-augmented systems where human cognition actively shapes machine adaptation through bidirectional feedback loops.

Our theoretical contribution formalizes CKICAS with fractal properties—self-similarity across scales, scale-invariant adaptation mechanisms, recursive decision architectures, and nested complexity boundaries—as the distinguishing feature enabling genuine intelligence augmentation.

Empirically, we validate CKICAS through a 6-month case study of Axiom-X, demonstrating fractal properties in a production intelligence system. External validation includes formal recognition from Niue and Tokelau governments for the Pacific constitutional AI project, plus endorsements from leading epidemiologists and health professionals (identities protected for privacy but verifiable by reviewers).

Practically, we extract design principles for implementing CKICAS in high-stakes domains including cybersecurity, emergency response, and sustainable development.

This research advances CAS theory by positioning intelligence not as emergent output but active input, with fractal properties enabling scale-invariant adaptation. The framework is validated through both systematic telemetry and real-world impact, providing actionable guidance for designing human-AI hybrid systems that maintain intelligence as a shaping force across all complexity scales.
